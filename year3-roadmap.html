BSc Psychology & Forensics — Final Year

Applied Forensic Psychology & Research Project

Stage 3 is where your degree comes fully into focus. You apply psychology directly to criminal justice contexts — offender behaviour, victimology, psychology in prisons, and the interface between mental health and the law. Your independent research project is the centrepiece of this year.

  • Forensic psychology in practice — courts, prisons, probation, secure settings
  • Offender rehabilitation and desistance theory
  • Victimology and trauma
  • Psychology of the legal system and expert witness practice
  • Independent research project — your dissertation
Your Dissertation — Make It Count

Choose a Topic That Opens Doors

Your dissertation is a talking point in every interview for the next five years. Choose a topic that directly intersects your two qualifications and your local context. Here are three strong options for your situation:

Option 1
Trauma-informed counselling approaches with offenders in community settings in West Wales
Option 2
Barriers to mental health support for people in contact with the criminal justice system in rural Wales
Option 3
The therapeutic relationship in forensic counselling — practitioner experiences in secure settings

⚠ Check Your BPS Accreditation Status

On completing your OU BSc, check whether your degree is accredited by the British Psychological Society (BPS). If it is, you will be eligible for Graduate Basis for Chartership (GBC) — the essential gateway to becoming a Chartered Psychologist and to postgraduate clinical or forensic psychology training. Contact the OU or check the BPS website at bps.org.uk to confirm your status immediately on graduating.

On Qualifying

Apply for BACP Membership Immediately

The moment you receive your diploma certificate, apply for BACP Registered Member status. This is the professional credential that appears on your CV and makes you eligible to practise ethically and independently. Do not delay this step.

  • Apply at bacp.co.uk as soon as you qualify
  • Begin planning for BACP Accreditation (450 post-qual hours)
  • Identify a post-qualification supervisor now
  • Get professional indemnity insurance if going private

Your BACP Accreditation Pathway

BACP Accreditation is the gold standard credential that unlocks senior NHS roles, higher private rates, and professional recognition. You cannot apply immediately — it requires post-qualification supervised hours. Here is your route:

Volunteer & Placement Work — Year 3: Go NHS Forensic

Year 3 placements should be as close as possible to your target employers. The aim is not just experience — it is to get a foot in the door at the organisations where you will be applying for paid roles in Summer 2029.

📍 In Carmarthen — Your Primary Target  |  ★ Most Important

Cwm Seren Low Secure Forensic Unit — Hywel Dda UHB

Cwm Seren is an 18-bed NHS Low Secure Forensic Unit based in Carmarthen, run by Hywel Dda University Health Board. This is the single most important organisation for your career. In Year 3, approach Hywel Dda's volunteering team directly and request a student placement, observer or volunteer support role within the unit or its adjacent Community Forensic Mental Health Team. The people you meet here will become your future referees and colleagues.

Role: Student Placement / Volunteer Observer / Therapy Support
Contact: Hywel Dda Volunteering — hduhb.nhs.wales/working-for-us/volunteering
Also contact: Hywel Dda Psychology Department directly
★ A volunteer role at Cwm Seren or the Community Forensic MH Team before you apply for paid roles here is the single most powerful thing you can do for your career in Year 3.
📍 ~25 miles — Swansea

Swansea Bay UHB — Forensic Mental Health

Swansea Bay University Health Board provides forensic mental health services for the wider South Wales population and works in close collaboration with Hywel Dda through the ARCH programme. Their Psychology department regularly takes student placements and advertises Band 4 associate practitioner roles — an ideal first paid step.

Role: Student placement / Band 4 associate (when qualifying)
Contact: sbuhb.nhs.wales/jobs

NACRO Cymru — Community Forensic

NACRO Cymru works with offenders in the community across West Wales, supporting resettlement and rehabilitation. Their community support worker roles are an excellent entry point into paid forensic work, often advertised at a level accessible immediately on qualifying.

Role: Volunteer then paid Resettlement / Support Worker

Applying for Your First Paid Roles

⚠ Start Applying Before You Fully Qualify

NHS recruitment moves slowly. Begin applying for Band 4 and Band 5 roles in Spring 2029 — three to four months before you formally qualify. NHS posts routinely have start dates 8–12 weeks after offer. If you wait until you hold your certificates in hand, you risk a gap between qualifying and starting work.
