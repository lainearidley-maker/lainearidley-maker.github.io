Why Year 1 Sets Everything Up

Year 1 is about laying solid groundwork simultaneously across three tracks: your OU degree, your counselling diploma, and real-world experience. Getting your DBS early, securing your first volunteer role, and stacking free OU OpenLearn certificates means you enter Year 2 with a CV that already stands out. Your location in Carmarthen is a genuine advantage — Hywel Dda University Health Board's Low Secure Forensic Unit (Cwm Seren) is right on your doorstep, and building local connections now pays dividends later.

Open University

Degree: OU Stage 1 Modules

BSc Psychology & Forensics — Year 1

What You Will Cover

Stage 1 introduces key psychological theories, research methods, and core debates across psychology and forensic practice. Every theory you learn here becomes a framework you will reference in client sessions and job interviews for the rest of your career.

  • Core psychological perspectives and major theoretical models
  • Introduction to research methods — qualitative and quantitative
  • Foundations of forensic psychology — crime, behaviour, justice
  • Biological, social and developmental psychology
Strategic Tip

Choose your dissertation topic early. Your final-year research project (Year 3) is a major talking point in every interview. Begin thinking now about a forensic or therapeutic angle — something like trauma in offender rehabilitation, or counselling outcomes in secure settings in West Wales. Plant the seed in Year 1.

Counselling Diploma

Diploma Year 1: Theory & Self-Awareness

Core Focus

Theoretical Models

Absorb the three major counselling orientations thoroughly. You will draw on all three throughout your career regardless of which you specialise in later.

  • Person-Centred Therapy (Rogers)
  • Cognitive Behavioural Therapy (CBT)
  • Psychodynamic Therapy (Freud / object relations)
Essential Action

Start Your Reflective Journal — Now

BACP accreditation requires substantial evidence of reflective practice. Starting your journal from day one means three years of evidence by the time you apply. Record every client session, every supervision, every moment of growth or difficulty.

Supervised Hours

Log Client Hours From Day One

Most diplomas require 100+ supervised client hours by qualification. Keep a meticulous log from your very first session — date, duration, client code, presenting issue, supervision received. This log is your evidence base for BACP accreditation post-qualification.

West Wales — Within 40 Miles of SA31

Volunteer Work — Year 1

Aim to secure at least one volunteer role by Christmas 2026. Two by the end of Year 1 is excellent. Prioritise getting your enhanced DBS immediately — most organisations cannot proceed without it and it can take several weeks to arrive.

📍 ~14 miles — Llanelli

Llanelli Mind

Llanelli Mind has provided mental health services across Carmarthenshire since 1986. Their counselling service spans the Swansea Valley and they offer structured volunteer roles in their Day Opportunities service — ideal for building core support skills alongside your diploma.

Role: Peer Support Volunteer / Day Opportunities Support
Commitment: Approx. 1 day per week
Contact: llanelli-mind.org.uk
📍 In Carmarthen — Your Doorstep

Glangwili Hospital Befriender

Volunteering Wales lists a Hospital Befriender opportunity at Glangwili General Hospital. You support patients who feel isolated, building therapeutic communication skills in a clinical environment right in your home town — and inside your future NHS employer's estate.

Role: Hospital Befriender / Patient Support Volunteer
Commitment: Flexible
Contact: carmarthenshire.volunteering-wales.net
📍 ~25 miles — Swansea

Samaritans — Swansea Branch

Samaritans has nine Welsh branches and Swansea is your nearest. This is gold-standard CV experience for any counselling career. The active listening skills you develop here directly mirror what your diploma teaches, and employers universally respect Samaritans experience on a CV.

Role: Listening Volunteer (telephone, face-to-face, email support)
Commitment: Minimum 1 year; flexible shifts including evenings and weekends
Contact: samaritans.org/branches — search Swansea
Also Action in Year 1

BACP Student Membership

Apply for BACP Student Membership as soon as you begin your diploma. It costs very little, signals professional seriousness to employers, gives you access to ethical frameworks and the BACP journal, and sets you on the path toward accreditation post-qualification.

Contact: bacp.co.uk/membership
