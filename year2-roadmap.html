Year 2 — Where Your Specialism Takes Shape

Year 1 gave you your foundations. Year 2 is where you begin to stand out. Your diploma moves from theory into serious client work, your OU modules go deeper into applied and forensic psychology, and your volunteer portfolio gains a distinctly forensic flavour. By the end of this year you should have over 100 supervised client hours, trauma-informed practice training under your belt, and direct experience inside a criminal justice or forensic-adjacent setting.

This is also the year to start making contacts inside Hywel Dda University Health Board and the local criminal justice system. The people you meet volunteering this year will often become your referees and your future colleagues.

BSc Psychology & Forensics — Year 2

Deepening Into Applied & Forensic Psychology

Stage 2 takes you into more sophisticated territory — research design, psychological assessment, and the application of psychology in real-world contexts including criminal justice. Your studies become directly relevant to the volunteer and placement work you are doing simultaneously.

  • Advanced research methods and psychological assessment
  • Psychology of criminal behaviour and offending
  • Social psychology and group dynamics
  • Applied forensic psychology in practice
  • Abnormal psychology and clinical frameworks

⚠ Dissertation Planning Starts Now

Your Year 3 research project requires a topic, literature review, methodology and ethics approval — all of which take time. During Year 2, begin reading around your chosen area. A dissertation on counselling outcomes in forensic settings, trauma-informed practice in West Wales, or offender rehabilitation will make every job interview richer and demonstrate genuine expertise to employers like Hywel Dda and HMP Swansea.

Counselling Diploma

Diploma Year 2: Into Serious Client Work

Primary Goal

Accumulate Supervised Client Hours

Year 2 is when your hour count builds substantially. Most diplomas require 100+ hours before qualification. Track every session rigorously and ensure you are receiving regular supervision — BACP accreditation requires documented evidence of both.

  • Minimum 50+ hours in Year 2 alone
  • Individual supervision every 2 weeks minimum
  • Group supervision where available
  • Personal therapy — most diplomas require this; keep receipts as evidence
Placement Strategy

Seek a Forensic-Flavoured Placement

Your diploma will arrange a placement for you, but you can influence where you go. Actively request a placement with any of the following types of organisations — each one brings your two qualifications together powerfully.

  • Addiction or substance misuse service
  • Domestic violence or abuse support service
  • Prison in-reach or through-the-gate service
  • Community mental health with complex needs clients

Welsh Language Note

Dysgu Cymraeg — Learn Welsh

All Hywel Dda University Health Board job postings include Welsh language desirability. Even basic Welsh (Level 1) is noted positively on applications and demonstrates commitment to working in West Wales. The Say Something in Welsh app and Dysgu Cymraeg (learnwelsh.cymru) are both free and flexible for self-study alongside your other commitments.

Volunteer Work — Year 2: Go Forensic

Year 2 is the time to push your volunteering into forensic and criminal justice territory. Alongside any roles you are continuing from Year 1, aim to add at least one placement with a direct forensic connection. This is what separates your CV from every other counselling student's.

📍 ~25 miles — Swansea  |  ★ Priority Placement

Forward Trust — Y Bont Programme, HMP Swansea

The Forward Trust runs Y Bont, a 12-week trauma-informed drug treatment and recovery programme inside HMP Swansea. This is the single most valuable forensic volunteer placement available to you within your radius. You gain direct prison environment experience, addiction counselling exposure, trauma-informed practice in action, and criminal justice context — all in one setting.

Role: Programme Support Volunteer
Commitment: Weekly; minimum 6 months recommended
Contact: forwardtrust.org.uk | via HMP Swansea Psychology Dept
⚠ Enhanced DBS and security clearance required — apply a minimum of 8 weeks before your intended start date. Begin this process in Summer 2027.
📍 ~25 miles — HMP Swansea

Pact — Prison Family Services

Pact runs the Family and Visitors Centre at HMP Swansea, providing emotional and practical support to families of prisoners. This is a highly specialist population that bridges social work, counselling and criminal justice in a single role.

Role: Family Services Volunteer
Commitment: Flexible
Contact: prison-advice.org.uk
📍 ~14 miles — Llanelli / Carmarthenshire

LINKS Mental Health Charity

LINKS supports adults with mental health needs across Carmarthenshire, including veterans and blue light workers. This is a forensic-adjacent population with complex histories — excellent for building experience with clients who have criminal justice connections.

Role: Support Volunteer — group activities, community outreach
Commitment: Flexible
Contact: links.uk.net
📍 Carmarthenshire-wide

Carmarthenshire Wellbeing Service

Carmarthenshire County Council runs a Wellbeing Service providing community mental health support across the county, working in close collaboration with Hywel Dda Health Board, probation, police and the third sector. Volunteering here gives you multi-agency experience and — critically — puts you inside the network of your future NHS employer from the inside.

Role: Volunteer Support Worker / Community Wellbeing Volunteer
Commitment: Flexible; approx. 1 day per week
Contact: carmarthenshire.gov.wales — Wellbeing Service
Building relationships inside Carmarthenshire County Council and Hywel Dda at this stage means you already know people when you apply for paid roles in Year 3 and beyond.
